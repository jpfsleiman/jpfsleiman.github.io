Hello

I am JohnPaul Sleiman, a PhD student at U of Rochester in the DRIP (Dirt, Rivers, Ice, Particles) Lab. I am currently trying to understand near surface processes on Mars, especially creep, and permafrost cycles on Earth. To do this I study geomorphology, near surface geophysics and sediment transport.

RESEARCH

My research interests are found within the field of geomorphology and geophysics, especially those fields that can help offer insight to other celestial bodies. This includes comparative planetary physics, oceanography, low temperature geophysics, geochemistry, fluid mechanics and sediment transport.

    A little about me

    Having graduated Furman University in 2021 where I studyed physics and geology I had a focus on Astronomy and Geochemistry. Trying to understand surface processes on our planet and other celestial bodies is my main goal. (see curernt and past research)

    Outside of research, I am a huge advocate of teaching public speaking, especially when it comes to teaching science. Understanding core scientific ideas is a needed skill, but being able to communicate to non scientists is even more needed. I support this ideal by being a JPL Solar system ambassador , a Speech and debate coach and a TA.

    I am also a whiskey lover, cook, antique car enthusiast and amateur astrophotographer. If you like to talk don't hesitate to reach out and contact me.
