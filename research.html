Hill Slope Failures and
Soil Displacement

As the climate warms, what happens to hillslopes with permafrost underneath? What changes occur in terrain that experiences seasonal frost heave? These experiments mimic temperature patterns from Arctic environments using indoor climate chambers to study frost heave and ice lens formation in controlled soil conditions. This research will help us understand how temperature influences hillslope stability in permafrost-rich environments.

Lobate Features On Mars

Solifluction lobes, large-scale soil patterns commonly observed on Earth's hillslopes in cold environments, form due to frost-heave processes. Recent studies have proposed that similar patterns found on Mars may be valuable paleoenvironmental indicators, but it remains unclear whether they form from the same icy processes as solifluction lobes on Earth.

Worked with Dr. David Moffett on Radio emission from Supernova Remnants

  1. Radio observations were conducted at P-band (224 – 480 MHz) and L-band (1 – 2 GHz) using the Very Large Array (VLA) of NRAO
  2. Extracted X-ray spectra for several sources using archival observations made by Chandra, Suzaku, and XMM- Newton.
  3. Applied synchrotron models to these spectra using constraints; namely, derived flux densities and spectral indices obtained from the L-Band and P-Band observations.
  4. Completed RFI excision, and calibration of bandpass, phase and flux density.
  5. Analyzed the long wavelength radio properties of known SNRs, specifically 3C 391, Kes 69, and W41.
  6. Successfully wrote and funded proposal for more telescope time to observation G28.6−0.1 and G32.4+0.1.
  7. Conducted data analysis on Green-Bank radio data (C-Band).

Worked with Dr. Christopher Romanek on my senior thesis

Magnesian Calcite as a Paleoenvironmental Indicator where we trying to use geochemical markers to determine the chemistry of Martain oceans. This work was funded by SC NASA Space Grant Consortium Research Grant.

  1. Synthesized calcium carbonate under unique environmental conditions using the chemostat technique.
  2. Used Rietveld refinement on XRD analysis to determine mol% Mg determined of lab grown calcite.
  3. Used a bulk digestion method to determine mol% Mg determined of lab grown calcite from an ICP-OES.
  4. Used FEG-SEM to look at crystal grains and determine overgrowth on lab grown calcite.
  5. Analyzed the long wavelength radio properties of known SNRs, specifically 3C 391, Kes 69, and W41.
  6. Installed and calibrated a Prodigy 7 ICP-OES
